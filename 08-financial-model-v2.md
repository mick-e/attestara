## 1. Revenue Streams

### Stream 1: Enterprise SaaS — Managed Prover Service + Enterprise SDK

The primary recurring revenue stream. Organisations pay for:
- **Enterprise SDK licence** — Annual subscription for production-grade implementation with HSM key management, compliance reporting, SLA-backed support, and advanced monitoring
- **Managed Prover Service** — Monthly SaaS fee for hosted ZK proof generation infrastructure; eliminates the need for enterprises to run their own prover hardware

This is the core business. Every enterprise deploying Attestara needs both components in production.

### Stream 2: Professional Services — Integration and Implementation Support

The second revenue stream, particularly important in the first 18 months when the reference implementation is new and enterprise buyers need hand-holding through first deployment.

Services include:
- **Pilot engagement** — Structured 90-day paid deployment in a sandboxed environment; validates the protocol for the customer and produces a case study for Attestara
- **Integration support** — Bespoke implementation assistance for organisations integrating with existing procurement systems, financial infrastructure, or agent frameworks
- **Custom credential schema design** — Designing Authority Credentials tailored to specific enterprise authority delegation structures
- **Advisory retainer** — Ongoing quarterly engagement for organisations with active agentic AI governance programmes

Professional services is not the long-term business — it is the land-and-expand motion that gets enterprises into the SaaS products and generates the case studies needed to close the next deal.

## 8. Funding Requirements

### Seed Round Target: €550,000–€750,000

The higher target vs earlier modelling reflects:
1. Professional services-heavy Y1 means slower initial revenue ramp
2. Two critical engineering hires needed in Y2 to build production SaaS
3. Professional services engagements require delivery capacity (founder time) before SaaS revenue scales

**Use of funds:**

| Category | Amount | % | Rationale |
|----------|--------|---|-----------|
| Product (ZK PoC → production SDK + contracts) | €200,000 | 28% | Core protocol build |
| Engineering hires (Y2 ramp) | €180,000 | 25% | ZK + SDK engineers for first 12 months |
| Legal (entity, IP, enforceability, audits) | €95,000 | 13% | ZK audit €60-80K is non-negotiable |
| GTM (pilots, events, content, outreach) | €75,000 | 10% | Pilot sales and case studies |
| Infrastructure | €50,000 | 7% | RPC, servers, tooling |
| Working capital / runway buffer | €120,000 | 17% | 3-month buffer beyond model |

**Runway:** €700,000 provides approximately 26–28 months to break-even trajectory, with buffer for sales cycle slippage.

## 9. Sensitivity Analysis

### Downside: Professional Services Converts Poorly (40% of base)

If pilot-to-SaaS conversion drops to 35% and average PS deal size is lower:

| | Y1 | Y2 | Y3 |
|-|----|----|-----|
| Revenue | €55,000 | €175,000 | €540,000 |
| Costs | €216,000 | €792,000 | €876,000 |
| EBITDA | (€161,000) | (€617,000) | (€336,000) |

In the downside case, €750,000 seed is insufficient. Would require delaying engineering hires or raising a bridge at Month 18. Mitigation: front-load pilot activity; target 3 signed pilots before seed closes.

### Upside: SI Partnership Activates (Month 18)

If a system integrator brings 8–10 enterprise customers in Year 2:

| | Y1 | Y2 | Y3 |
|-|----|----|-----|
| Revenue | €120,000 | €680,000 | €2,100,000 |
| Costs | €216,000 | €850,000 | €980,000 |
| EBITDA | (€96,000) | (€170,000) | €1,120,000 |

In the upside case, break-even occurs in Y2 Q4. Series A from a position of strong ARR.

### Key Risk Factors

| Risk | Impact on Revenue | Mitigation |
|------|------------------|------------|
| Enterprise sales cycles longer than 5 months | Delays Y2 SaaS ramp by 1-2 quarters | Pilot-first reduces barrier; PS provides bridge revenue |
| Smart contract audit delays SDK launch | Defers Y2 SaaS to Y3 | Begin audit process Month 8; use testnet for pilots |
| Engineering hires delayed/expensive | Increases Y2 burn | Begin recruiting Month 10; ZK talent market is competitive |
| Competitor enters market (Salesforce) | Reduces Y3 conversion rate | Accelerate standards body positioning; lock in early customers |
| ZK PoC fails performance threshold | Delays entire product | Alternative proof systems identified; not a show-stopper |
