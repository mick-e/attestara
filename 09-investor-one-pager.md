## The Problem

AI agents are now negotiating contracts, committing to supply agreements, and executing financial transactions on behalf of enterprises — without any infrastructure to prove they had authority to do so.

When an AI agent makes a binding commitment that causes harm, nobody knows who is accountable. When two AI agents from competing companies negotiate, neither party can verify the other's agent had authority to agree to what it agreed. When regulators ask for the audit trail, there isn't one.

This is not a theoretical future problem. **75% of UK financial services firms are already deploying AI.** The EU AI Act's Article 9 obligations for high-risk agentic systems take effect August 2026. The UK Treasury Select Committee declared in January 2026 that regulators are currently "exposing consumers and the financial system to potentially serious harm."

The infrastructure layer for trusted agentic AI doesn't exist yet.

---

## The Insight

In 1832, thirty-one competing London banks faced a structurally identical problem: how do you enable daily transactions between parties who don't trust each other, with no single authority enforcing the rules?

Their solution — the London Bankers' Clearing House — didn't make the bankers trustworthy. It made their commitments enforceable regardless of trust.

**Attestara is the London Clearing House for the AI agent economy.**

---

## The Solution

Attestara is an open cryptographic trust protocol enabling AI agents to negotiate, commit, and be held accountable across organisational boundaries — without either party trusting the other's assertions.

**How it works:**

1. **Verifiable Identity** — Each agent has a cryptographically-verifiable Decentralised Identifier (DID) registered on-chain, binding it to its deploying organisation

2. **Enforceable Authority** — Organisations issue signed Authority Credentials to their agents, encoding their negotiating mandate. Zero-Knowledge proofs allow agents to prove they're operating within mandate without revealing sensitive parameters

3. **Binding Commitment** — Smart contracts record final agreements as tamper-proof, auditable commitment records — constituting the evidence required for regulatory compliance and legal accountability

4. **Dual Governance** — Supports both open DAO governance (for multi-party ecosystems) and bilateral P2P deployment (for regulated enterprises that cannot participate in open DAOs)
